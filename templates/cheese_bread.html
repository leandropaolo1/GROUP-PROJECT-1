Brazilian Pão de queijo: The staple cheese bread

❮

What are the main ingredients?

Pão de queijo is a popular Brazilian snack made of cassava flour, eggs, cheese, milk, and oil.

❯
❮

How was it created?

The history of Pão de queijo dates back to the 18th century in the state of Minas Gerais, Brazil, where it was originally made by African slaves who used cassava flour as a substitute for wheat flour.

❯
❮

When should you have it?

You can find it being used in all meals. It is commonly served in cafes, bakeries, and restaurants, as well as at home, where it is often made from scratch using family recipes passed down through generations.
