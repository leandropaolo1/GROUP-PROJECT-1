Pão de queijo

Pão de queijo is a traditional Brazilian snack that is popular throughout the country. The name translates to "cheese bread" in English. Pão de queijo is often served as a snack or appetizer, and it can be found in cafes, bakeries, and restaurants throughout Brazil.

Feijão Tropeiro

Feijão Tropeiro is a national Brazilian dish, originally from the state of Minas Gerais, but popular throughout the country. It is a hearty and flavorful bean dish made with black beans, bacon, sausage, eggs, onions, garlic, and manioc flour (also known as cassava flour).

Beef Feijoada

Beef Feijoada is a variation of the traditional Brazilian dish, Feijoada, which is typically made with black beans, pork meat, and sausage. In the Beef Feijoada recipe, beef is used instead of pork to create a hearty and flavorful dish.
